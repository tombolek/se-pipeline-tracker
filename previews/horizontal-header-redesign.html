Proposed layout

Cross-cutting tools (Quick Note, Recent Actions, What's New, How To, profile) move to a horizontal header above everything. The sidebar keeps its navigation role; its footer shrinks to just the connection indicator.

  • Full app name fits on one line — less visual load.
  • Feature chips are scannable horizontally instead of stacked.
  • The sidebar regains vertical space for nav as the product grows.
  • Unread counts (What's New 2) render inline, no cramped column.
  • Connection indicator stays in the sidebar footer where the offline story lives — small, purposeful, out of the top chrome.

Note: the How To button can stay pinned inside the header, but clicking still routes to /settings/how-to; nothing changes behavior-wise, only placement.

Sidebar is now 52px narrower (was 224, is 208) — reclaim to the main canvas. Header is a fixed 56px tall, sticky to the viewport.

C. What's New & Recent actions keep their badges

Unread counts sit inline next to the button label. No change to the underlying data — it's the same count that was in the sidebar before.

D. Where the connection indicator goes

My recommendation: keep it in the sidebar footer (not in the top header). Rationale:

  • Header should stay calm — it's for features, not status.
  • The indicator is small, passive most of the time (green dot), and only demands attention when offline or syncing — exactly like the nav item badges.
  • When the user needs to click through to pending-sync items, it's one click; one extra icon in the top bar isn't worth it.

Alternative: replace one of the four feature chips with a small dot indicator in the top bar — but then the sidebar footer becomes empty entirely and we lose a natural home for the offline story. Not recommended.
