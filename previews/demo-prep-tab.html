Q1

What initiative are we anchoring to?

EU Data Act compliance program driving enterprise-wide MDM modernization

Strong
Answer

Deutsche Telekom is undergoing an EU Data Act compliance program that requires them to modernize their master data governance across all business units. This is not a "nice to have" — it's a regulatory mandate with a 2027 deadline. The initiative is sponsored by the Group CDO and is part of their broader digital sovereignty strategy.

Evidence
● Note (Mar 28): "CDO confirmed EU Data Act is the primary driver — need governance framework across 12 BUs by Q2 2027"
● MEDDPICC Need: "Enterprise MDM governance to meet EU Data Act requirements"
● SE Comments: "Regulatory timeline is firm — they cannot miss this"

Demo tip: Open with: "You told us the EU Data Act is driving a governance overhaul across 12 BUs by Q2 2027. Today we'll show exactly how that gets done." — This immediately proves you listened.

Q2

What is the primary pain we are addressing?

No unified view of data quality across 12 BUs — each team has its own standards and tools

Strong
Answer

DT has 12 business units each running their own data quality tools and standards. There is no single pane of glass for the CDO to see data quality posture across the group. Audit findings in Q4 2025 flagged this as a material risk. They estimate 30% of operational decisions are based on data they can't fully trust.

Evidence
● MEDDPICC Pain: "Fragmented DQ tooling, no cross-BU visibility, audit risk"
● Note (Apr 2): "Audit findings from Q4 cited lack of unified DQ reporting as material weakness"

Demo tip: Before showing the product, display a "broken world" slide: 12 disconnected tools → CDO has zero visibility → audit flagged it. Let them nod before you touch the UI.

Q3

What is the single objective of this demo?

Partially clear — multiple themes mentioned, needs sharpening

Partial
Answer

Notes mention showing DQ monitoring, MDM catalog, and governance workflows — that's three topics, not one objective. The demo risks becoming a feature tour. A clear single objective could be: "Show how a CDO gets real-time, cross-BU data quality visibility from a single console — the exact thing their audit flagged as missing."

Evidence
● Note (Apr 5): "Plan to show DQ dashboards + catalog + governance workflow in 45min" — too broad
● No single objective defined in any task or next-step description

Action needed — sharpen the objective

Ask the AE or champion: "If they could only remember one thing from the demo, what should it be?" Then cut everything that doesn't serve that one takeaway. Remember: clarity beats coverage.

Q4

What job are we solving?

Unified DQ monitoring across all 12 BUs with automated rule propagation

Strong
Answer

The CDO's team needs to define DQ rules once and enforce them across 12 BUs automatically, then see compliance in a single dashboard. Today this is done manually via spreadsheets and email — it takes weeks per rule change.

Evidence
● MEDDPICC Decision Criteria: "Centralized rule management with BU-level compliance tracking"

Demo tip: Name the job before you touch the UI: "The job is: define once, enforce everywhere, see compliance instantly." Then show the three steps. We demo outcomes, not screens.

Q5

What is the impact if this works?

Regulatory risk mentioned but no quantified business impact

Partial
Answer

We know the regulatory risk is real (EU Data Act non-compliance penalties), but we don't have the numbers. No one has quantified what "bad data decisions" actually cost DT. Without explicit impact numbers, the demo will compete on features rather than outcomes.

What we know
● Risk reduced: Regulatory non-compliance (EU Data Act penalties)
What's missing
● Cost avoided: No estimate of manual DQ effort cost or audit remediation spend
● Revenue enabled: No data on revenue impacted by poor data quality

Action needed — quantify the impact

Ask the champion: "What did the last DQ issue actually cost — in hours, in delayed decisions, in audit findings?" Even a rough number transforms the demo close. If we don't translate, we compete on features.

Q6

What commitment are we asking for?

Not defined — no clear ask or next step after the demo

Missing
Assessment

There is no defined commitment or next step planned for after the demo. Without this, the demo becomes a presentation — not a deal-advancing event. A good demo always moves the deal forward.

Suggested commitments for this stage
Shortlist confirmation
Validation workshop
Executive alignment meeting

Critical gap — define the ask before you demo

Align with the AE now: "What specifically do we want them to agree to at the end of this demo?" Based on the Develop Solution stage, a validation workshop or PoC scope agreement would be appropriate. End the demo with a clear before/after, then make the ask.

Overall Assessment

This deal is at D2 (Informed Demo) level — you have confirmed the initiative and primary pain, which puts you ahead of most demos at this stage. However, two critical gaps will hold you back:

1. The demo objective is unfocused — three topics in 45 minutes guarantees a shallow, forgettable session. Pick one story thread and go deep.
2. No defined commitment — without an ask, the demo ends with "thanks, we'll get back to you." Align with the AE on a concrete next step before you present.
